Briefly introduce to Laser TURP (HOLEP) surgery

HOLEP (Holmium LASER Enucleation of Prostate) is a minimally invasive procedure designed to treat benign prostatic hyperplasia (BPH) or an enlarged prostate. This modern approach uses advanced laser technology to remove excess prostate tissue, helping men experience relief from urinary symptoms.

What is Benign prostatic hyperplasia (BPH)?

Benign prostatic hyperplasia, commonly known as BPH, is a condition that affects many men as they age because of hormonal imbalance and blocks the urinary passage.

BPH symptoms

  • Difficult to initiate urination
  • Poor or weak urinary stream
  • Strain to pass urine
  • Sense of incomplete emptying of urine
  • Frequent urination
  • Frequent night urination
  • Severe urge to pass urine
  • Urinary leakage
  • Frequent urinary infection
  • Blood in urine

How Laser TURP Works

LASER TURP uses a high-powered laser to remove excess prostate tissue, which helps relieve pressure on the urethra, improving urinary symptoms. The procedure is typically quicker and has a faster recovery time compared to traditional methods.

Who is a Candidate for LASER TURP?

Men who are experiencing moderate to severe BPH symptoms, especially those unresponsive to medication, may be ideal candidates for LASER TURP.

Risks Involved

LASER TURP is safe for heart patient on blood thinners, very elderly patients and patients with other medical conditions.
